What's your thinking style?

Thinking and Mindset

June 26th 2015

Anthony F. Gregorc, Ph. D. started research on thinking or learning style in 1969. A model was developed in 1984. Through his work he has categorized perceptual quality to concrete and abstract and ordering ability to sequential and random. He says all individuals posses a part of all these qualities. Some use one more than the other comfortably. Over a period of time these qualities might change in an individual.The abilities in combination are listed below.

  1. Concrete Sequential
  2. Abstract Sequential
  3. Concrete Random
  4. Abstract Random
If you would like to take the test please refer link Here.

The quiz result described my thinking style as Abstarct Random. I must say after reading the description I relate to 9 out of 10 of the characteristics. I thrive in group environment and build relationships easily. Also, I am sensitive towards others, friendly and collborative. I usually like to plan and finish my tasks in time to be able to focus on extra-curricular or social activities. I can learn best when I have the ability to be creative with my work. I feel all the above describe me well and perfectly fit into the Abstarct Random thinking style. DBC is a place where every individual has the ability to be creative, and help each other. Considering its a shared environment, I know it will benefit me. DBC allows me to be creative and independent as well. The pair programming concept will benefit my personality. Although, my personality reacts poor to criticism. I believe I take things personally, so this criticism can be converted to a constructive one. This personality is described to struggle with perfectionists, I feel it was something I sturggled with when I was youger. Over time I have developed patience and I handle perfectionists well. So, Phase-0 until now has been great with the collborative learning style. It has enbaled me to understand concepts well and work even more efficient.I feel I have struggled with extensive reading but I like to learn by watching videos more than reading so I try to alternate. I haven't yet faced a competitive environment or felt pressurized yet in order for me to guage.

Fixed Versus Growth Mindset

I was having a conversation about fixed and growth mindset with a few friends just before I came across the article by DBC. It was quite interesting as I was just understanding that I have many traits of the fixed mindset category. I feel it is good to have a balance of both. One should keep an open outlook to have a constructive future. Considering I am Abstract Random thinker, I automatically fall under many of the fixed mindset characteristics.For example, I have difficulty in accepting negetive feedback. So I used to ignore it. Last couple of years, I have identitfied this and have been working towards using any criticism as a contructive feedback. As a kid I have been very pesimistic and I am trying to have a postivie outlook towards failures. I have a competitive behaviour and feel thretened by competition. I should take it at stride and make it healthy. Moreover, these days success stories and successful people around me make me feel inspired rather than dejected. Although, I wonder how strange it is that I love collborative atmosphere yet fear competition. As a first step, I have embraced my personality the way it is before I work towards being all-rounded.I feel setting a limit will shun growth and knowledge and growth should be boundless. When a person does not know their limit they keeps trying to outdo themselves. Setting a limit for onself will make them plateau eventually. This is something I am trying to work on.

I hope understanding my thinking and my mindset helps me become adaptive and enables me to have boundless growth. To understand your mindset, please feel free to read through this.
