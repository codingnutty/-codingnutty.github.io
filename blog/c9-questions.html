Make them respond!

Aug 12 2015

Have troubles expressing your questions? Finding it difficult to ask succinct questions? I often do. I tend to write a elaborate explanations to the questions that I would like to ask. Sometimes you may not find the time for it. Asking a good short questions consisting of just what is needed will do it and you will get a a good answer. The things that you must address before the question is out there for everyone to read are as follows:

  • READ ENOUGH : First it is a must to try and look for solutions at first. Stack overflow often has questions that are repeated and people sometimes reply with a link to another post on the same website. Hence, it mandatory to take some effort and look up the solution on your own.
  • CLEAR and CONCISE : The question should be a whole easy to understand sentance containing important keywords. Using just a couple of words to explain your thoughts assuming it is concise is not the way to go. For a coding question, include the language, its version, what is the desired outcome and what is the current outcome. Also, include things that your may have tried. Sometimes a picture of the result explains everything.
  • PRROF READ : Proof read your post before actually posting it and make sure it is easy to understand. Spelling mistakes will create a bad impression and people may not want to reply.
  • CAPTIONS : Use full and easy to understand captions instead of being vague. eg:Bad: android if else problems
    Good: Why does str == "value" evaluate to false when str is set to "value"?

It is important to make sure that apart from writing proper questions one should be able to frame quick quesions with right content while in person. For example you are at work and you want to ask your manager a quick question.You can include projectname,Any error message, a tag for the language, library, and specific API your question relates to.

Last but not the least, there is loads of information out there that help you ask the right question at the right place. There is nothing to be afraid of while asking questions even if you feel they are stupid but it is important to think before you ask.
